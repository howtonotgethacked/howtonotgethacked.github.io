Essential security tips and practices to protect yourself in the digital world. Follow these guidelines to stay safe online and protect your personal information.

üîê Password Security

  • Use unique passwords for every account
  • Create passwords with at least 12 characters
  • Include uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols
  • Use a reputable password manager
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) everywhere
  • Never share passwords or write them down
  • Change passwords immediately if breached

üìß Email Security

  • Verify sender identity before clicking links
  • Check URLs carefully for misspellings
  • Never provide sensitive info via email
  • Use email encryption for sensitive communications
  • Be suspicious of urgent or threatening messages
  • Don't download unexpected attachments
  • Report phishing attempts to your IT team

üåê Web Browsing

  • Only visit HTTPS websites for sensitive activities
  • Keep browsers and plugins updated
  • Use ad blockers and privacy extensions
  • Clear cookies and browsing data regularly
  • Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities
  • Use private/incognito mode when appropriate
  • Verify website certificates and URLs

üì± Mobile Security

  • Keep your device OS and apps updated
  • Only download apps from official stores
  • Review app permissions before installing
  • Use device lock screens with strong PINs
  • Enable remote wipe capabilities
  • Avoid connecting to unknown Wi-Fi networks
  • Use mobile VPN for public networks

üíª Computer Security

  • Install security updates immediately
  • Use reputable antivirus software
  • Enable automatic screen locks
  • Encrypt your hard drive
  • Regular backup important data
  • Use standard user accounts, not admin
  • Disable unnecessary services and ports

üè† Home Network

  • Change default router passwords
  • Use WPA3 encryption for Wi-Fi
  • Hide your network name (SSID)
  • Keep router firmware updated
  • Use guest networks for visitors
  • Monitor connected devices regularly
  • Disable WPS and unnecessary features

üö® If You've Been Compromised

Take these immediate steps if you suspect your accounts or devices have been compromised:

1. Immediate Actions

  • Disconnect from the internet
  • Change all passwords
  • Enable 2FA on all accounts
  • Check account activity logs

2. Assess the Damage

  • Check bank and credit card statements
  • Review recent account activities
  • Look for unauthorized purchases
  • Check credit reports

3. Report and Recover

  • Contact your bank and credit card companies
  • File reports with relevant authorities
  • Contact credit bureaus
  • Document everything

4. Prevent Future Attacks

  • Update all software and systems
  • Run full antivirus scans
  • Review and improve security practices
  • Monitor accounts closely
